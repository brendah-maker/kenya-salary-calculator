How PAYE is Calculated in Kenya (2026 Guide)

Updated 2026 • Income Tax & Payroll Guide

PAYE (Pay As You Earn) is the income tax deducted directly from an employee’s salary by the employer and remitted to the Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA). It is one of the most important statutory deductions affecting your monthly take-home pay.

Understanding how PAYE is calculated helps you verify your payslip, plan your finances, and avoid confusion about tax deductions. Below is a clear step-by-step breakdown of how PAYE works in Kenya.

1. Determine Your Taxable Income

PAYE is calculated based on your taxable income. This includes:

  • Basic salary
  • Allowances (house, commuter, hardship, etc.)
  • Bonuses and commissions
  • Other taxable benefits

From your gross pay, certain statutory deductions are allowed before calculating PAYE. These include:

  • NSSF contributions (6% of pensionable pay, capped)
  • Affordable Housing Levy (1.5% of gross pay)
  • SHIF (2.75% of gross pay)

Taxable Income = Gross Pay − Allowable Deductions

2. Apply the KRA Monthly Tax Bands (2026)

Kenya uses a progressive tax system, meaning different portions of your income are taxed at different rates.

  • 10% on the first KSh 24,000
  • 25% on the next KSh 8,333
  • 30% on the next KSh 467,667
  • 32.5% on the next KSh 300,000
  • 35% on amounts above KSh 800,000

This means your salary is not taxed at one flat rate — it is taxed progressively across these bands.

3. Calculate Gross PAYE

To calculate gross PAYE, apply each tax rate only to the portion of income within that specific band, then add them together.

The total tax from all applicable bands equals your Gross PAYE.

4. Subtract Personal Relief

Every resident employee in Kenya qualifies for a monthly personal relief of KSh 2,400.

Net PAYE Payable = Gross PAYE − 2,400

If the calculated PAYE is less than KSh 2,400, then no PAYE is payable.

Example Calculation (Simplified)

Suppose an employee has a taxable income of KSh 50,000.

  • 10% of 24,000 = 2,400
  • 25% of 8,333 = 2,083.25
  • 30% of remaining 17,667 = 5,300.10

Gross PAYE = 2,400 + 2,083.25 + 5,300.10 = KSh 9,783.35

Less Personal Relief (2,400)

Net PAYE = KSh 7,383.35

When Is PAYE Paid?

Employers are required to deduct PAYE from employees’ salaries and remit it to the KRA by the 9th day of the following month.

Failure to remit on time attracts penalties and interest.

Why Understanding PAYE Matters

Knowing how PAYE works allows you to:

  • Verify payroll deductions
  • Plan your net income accurately
  • Understand how salary increases affect taxation
  • Prepare for annual tax returns

Final Thoughts

PAYE calculation may seem complex at first, but once broken down into taxable income, tax bands, and reliefs, it becomes easier to understand.
