Changes to NSSF Contribution Laws in Kenya – 2026

The National Social Security Fund (NSSF) in Kenya has undergone significant reforms under the NSSF Act of 2013, transitioning from a flat-rate provident fund to a tiered, earnings-related pension scheme. As of February 1, 2026, the implementation has entered its fourth phase, bringing several important changes for employees and employers alike.

1. Revised Earnings Limits

The contribution rate remains at 12% of pensionable earnings, shared equally between the employee and employer (6% each). However, the earnings bands have been updated:

  • Tier I (Lower Earnings Limit): Increased to KES 9,000 (up from KES 8,000 in 2025)
  • Tier II (Upper Earnings Limit): Increased to KES 108,000 (up from KES 72,000 in 2025)

2. Impact on Monthly Deductions

  • Maximum Deduction: Employees earning KES 108,000 or more now contribute KES 12,960 per month (KES 6,480 each from employer and employee), up from KES 8,640 in 2025.
  • Lower Earners: For a worker earning exactly KES 9,000, the mandatory Tier I deduction is KES 540, matched by the employer.
  • Middle Earners: If your salary is between the old and new limits (e.g., KES 50,000), your deduction may remain at 6% of gross salary unchanged from previous rates.

3. The “Opt-Out” Clause for Tier II

Employers can still “contract out” of Tier II contributions. Key points:

  • Tier I: Contributions must always be remitted directly to NSSF.
  • Tier II: With approval from the Retirement Benefits Authority (RBA), funds can be redirected to a private, registered pension scheme.

4. Future Adjustments (2027 and Beyond)

2026 marks the final stage of the four-year phased transition. From February 2027 onward, any adjustments to contribution limits will be determined and announced via Gazette Notices by the Cabinet Secretary for Labour and Social Protection.

Conclusion

The 2026 NSSF reforms ensure a more equitable, earnings-related pension system while maintaining mandatory contributions for all employees. Employees and employers should review these changes carefully to plan contributions and retirement savings effectively.
