About Kenya Payroll Deductions

Kenyan employees have several statutory deductions from their salary, including PAYE (income tax), NSSF, SHIF, and Housing Levy. Understanding these helps you plan your finances effectively.

Kenya PAYE Tax Bands 2026

First KES 24,000 – 10%

KES 24,001 – 32,333 – 25%

KES 32,334 – 47,667 – 30%

KES 47,668 – 100,000 – 32.5%

Above KES 100,000 – 35%

Personal Relief: KES 2,400 per month

Understanding Deductions

PAYE is a progressive income tax deducted by employers. Higher income bands are taxed at higher rates. A monthly personal relief of KES 2,400 is deducted after calculation.

NSSF is a mandatory pension scheme. Employees contribute 6% of pensionable income (capped), and employers match it.

SHIF (Social Health Insurance Fund) replaced NHIF. Employees contribute 2.75% of gross salary with a minimum threshold. Funds support universal healthcare services.

The Housing Levy is 1.5% of gross salary. Employers match the employee’s contribution to support affordable housing initiatives.
