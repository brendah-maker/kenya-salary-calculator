Understanding Salary Deductions in Kenya

When you earn a salary in Kenya, several statutory deductions apply before you receive your net pay. These include PAYE (income tax), NSSF pension contribution, SHIF/SHA health insurance, and the Housing Levy. Understanding how these are calculated helps you plan your finances better.

PAYE is a progressive income tax deducted by employers. Higher income bands are taxed at higher rates. After calculating tax using tax bands, a monthly personal relief of KES 2,400 is deducted.

NSSF is a mandatory pension scheme. Employees contribute 6% of pensionable income (capped). Employers match the contribution. This helps provide retirement benefits.

SHIF (Social Health Insurance Fund) replaced NHIF. Employees contribute 2.75% of gross salary, with a minimum contribution threshold. Funds support universal healthcare services.

The Housing Levy is 1.5% of gross salary. Employers match the employee’s contribution. Funds are used to support Kenya’s affordable housing initiative.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is net salary calculated?
Net salary = Gross salary minus PAYE, NSSF, SHIF, and Housing Levy.

Does this calculator use 2026 tax rates?
Yes, it reflects the current Kenya payroll structure.

Can employers use this tool?
