NSSF Contribution Structure (Effective Feb 2026)

Total Rate: 12% of pensionable income (6% Employee + 6% Employer)

  • Tier I (LEL): 6% of earnings up to KES 9,000 (Max employee: KES 540)
  • Tier II (UEL): 6% of earnings between KES 9,001 and KES 108,000 (Max employee: KES 5,940)
  • Maximum Employee Deduction: KES 6,480
  • Total Combined Deduction (Employee + Employer): Up to KES 12,960 for income ≥ KES 108,000

SHIF Overview

Mandatory for all Kenyan residents earning a salary.

  • SHIF is designed to be more comprehensive and equitable, aimed at enhancing coverage for both formal and informal sectors.
  • Minimum Contribution: KES 300/month
  • 2.75% of gross monthly salary for salaried employees
  • Covers essential services, including outpatient care, inpatient services, maternity, dialysis, cancer management, and surgical services
  • Remittance by 9th of following month

PAYE Tax Bands 2026

  • KES 0 – 24,000: 10%
  • KES 24,001 – 32,333: 25%
  • KES 32,334 – 47,667: 30%
  • KES 47,668 – 100,000: 32.5%
  • KES 100,001 and above: 35%

Personal relief: KES 2,400/month

Value Added Tax (VAT)

  • Value Added Tax (VAT) is an indirect tax on goods and services at each stage of production and sale
  • It is collected by businesses but ultimately paid by the final consumer
  • Businesses pay VAT on purchases (input tax) and charge VAT on sales (output tax)
  • VAT payable is calculated as output tax minus input tax.
  • VAT applies to taxable goods, services, and imports.
